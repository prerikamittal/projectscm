Champaran Mutton - Smoky Clay Pot Goat Curry

Authentic Champaran Mutton Recipe

Champaran Mutton, also known as Ahuna Mutton, is a slow-cooked dish from Bihar prepared in a sealed clay pot. It features marinated goat meat cooked with whole spices, garlic, and mustard oil for a rich, smoky flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg mutton (goat meat)
  • 1 cup mustard oil
  • 2 whole garlic bulbs (peeled)
  • 2 onions, finely sliced
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tsp ginger paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, black cardamom)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat mustard oil until smoking. Let it cool slightly.
  2. Marinate mutton with all ingredients including oil and whole spices. Let it sit for 1–2 hours.
  3. Place the marinated mutton in a clay pot or heavy-bottomed pan. Seal with dough or foil to trap steam.
  4. Cook on low heat for 2–3 hours, shaking the pot occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Once the meat is tender and oil separates, open the seal and garnish with fresh coriander.
  6. Serve hot with plain rice, roti, or litti.
