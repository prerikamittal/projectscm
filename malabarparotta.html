Flaky Malabar Parotta Recipe

Malabar Parotta is a famous layered flatbread from Kerala, known for its flaky texture. It's soft inside, crispy outside, and best served with kurma or curry.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp oil + more for cooking
  • Water (as needed for kneading)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, salt, sugar, and 2 tbsp oil.
  2. Gradually add water and knead to make a soft, smooth dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1-2 hours.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls.
  5. Roll each ball very thin, apply some oil, and pleat (fold like a paper fan).
  6. Roll the pleated dough into a spiral and flatten slightly.
  7. Roll out gently into a disc shape without losing the layers.
  8. Heat a tawa (griddle) and cook the parotta with some oil until golden brown on both sides.
  9. After cooking, fluff the parotta by gently clapping it between your palms (optional for layers).
  10. Serve hot with vegetable kurma, chicken curry, or salna!
