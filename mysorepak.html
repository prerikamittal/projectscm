Classic Mysore Pak Recipe

Mysore Pak is a rich, melt-in-the-mouth Indian sweet made with ghee, sugar, and gram flour. It originated in the royal kitchens of Mysore Palace.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Grease a plate or tray with ghee and set aside.
  2. Sieve the besan to remove any lumps.
  3. Heat a tablespoon of ghee in a pan and lightly roast the besan until aromatic (do not brown).
  4. In another pan, dissolve sugar and water to make a one-string sugar syrup.
  5. Quickly add the roasted besan into the syrup while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  6. Start adding ghee little by little while stirring. The mixture will absorb the ghee and become porous.
  7. When the mixture leaves the sides of the pan and becomes frothy, pour it immediately onto the greased plate.
  8. Level it with a spatula and let it cool for a few minutes. Cut into squares and serve!
