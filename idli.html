Idli - Steamed Rice Cake

Classic Idli Recipe

Idli is a traditional South Indian breakfast made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. Soft, fluffy, and healthy, it's often served with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Water (for batter consistency)
  • Salt to taste
  • Ghee (clarified butter) for greasing the molds

Instructions:

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked mixture into a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Allow the batter to ferment for 8-12 hours in a warm place. The batter should rise and become bubbly.
  4. Grease idli molds with ghee.
  5. Fill the molds with the fermented batter and steam for 10-15 minutes until the idlis are soft and cooked through.
  6. Serve the idlis with coconut chutney and sambar for a classic South Indian breakfast!

Sambar

Classic Sambar Recipe

Sambar is a savory lentil soup made with tamarind, vegetables, and a special blend of spices. It is an essential accompaniment to Idli and Dosa.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup carrots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup potatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp sambar powder
  • 1 tsp tamarind paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Mustard seeds, curry leaves, and dried red chilies for tempering

Instructions:

  1. Wash the toor dal and cook in a pressure cooker until soft.
  2. In a pan, sauté onions and tomatoes until softened. Add carrots and potatoes, and cook until tender.
  3. Once the dal is cooked, add the sautéed vegetables and mix well.
  4. Add tamarind paste, sambar powder, and salt. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. In a separate pan, heat oil and temper mustard seeds, curry leaves, and dried red chilies. Pour this tempering over the sambar.
  6. Serve hot with idlis, dosas, or rice!

Coconut Chutney

Classic Coconut Chutney Recipe

Coconut chutney is a creamy, tangy, and slightly spicy chutney made with grated coconut, green chilies, and a tempering of mustard seeds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1/4 tsp ginger
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine grated coconut, green chilies, ginger, and salt. Add a little water to blend into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a small pan and temper mustard seeds and curry leaves.
  3. Pour the tempering over the chutney and mix well.
  4. Serve with idlis, dosas, or sambar!
