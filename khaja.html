Khaja - Sweet and Flaky Delight

Classic Khaja Recipe

Khaja is a layered, flaky pastry soaked in sugar syrup, making it an irresistible dessert in Indian cuisine. It is especially popular in the state of Odisha and Bihar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Oil (for frying)
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, ghee, and salt to form a soft dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll the dough into thin sheets and layer them one over another.
  3. Cut the layered dough into desired shapes and deep fry until golden and crispy.
  4. Prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until it reaches a single-thread consistency.
  5. Dip the fried pastries in the sugar syrup and let them soak for a few minutes.
  6. Allow them to cool before serving this delectable treat!
