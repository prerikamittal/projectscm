Litti Chokha - Bihari Delight

Traditional Litti Chokha Recipe

Litti Chokha is a rustic and flavorful dish from Bihar, made with stuffed whole wheat dough balls (Litti) and mashed spiced vegetables (Chokha). It is traditionally cooked over coal or wood fire and served with ghee.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup roasted gram flour (sattu)
  • 1 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1 tsp nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 2 tbsp mustard oil
  • 2-3 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 inch ginger (grated)
  • 1-2 green chilies (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Ghee for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough using whole wheat flour, salt, and water. Keep it aside covered.
  2. In a bowl, mix sattu, mustard oil, ajwain, kalonji, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and salt. This is the filling for the litti.
  3. Divide the dough, flatten each ball, fill it with sattu mixture, and seal it well.
  4. Bake the littis in a preheated oven at 200°C (390°F) for 30-35 minutes or cook them over charcoal/fire until golden brown.
  5. For chokha: roast eggplant, tomatoes, and boil potatoes. Mash them with mustard oil, garlic, salt, and green chili.
  6. Serve the hot littis with generous ghee and chokha on the side for a wholesome Bihari meal!
